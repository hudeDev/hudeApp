Uranus is the seventh Planet, its average distance to the Sun measures 2.87 billion km = 19.2 AU. On the teaching path, it is 2,870 meters near the sun. Uranus has a diameter of 52.3 thousand km, which equals 5,2 cm on the path. Its mass is 14 times greater than the earths. Uranus is 19 times further away from the sun than Earth and with the naked eye almost impossible to detect, so it is outside of the limit of visibility on our planet instructional path. Moons: 27.

Der Durchmesser des „Ringplaneten” ist 120.700 km, im Maßstab werden 12,1 cm daraus. Als zweitgrößter, gasförmiger Planet weist Saturn die 95,15fache Erdmasse auf. The temperature is due to the distance from the Sun, in permanent frost depth range: -156 ° C to -212 ° C have been measured. A year on Uranus takes 84.01 years, one day 17 hours, 14 minutes. The atmosphere of this gas giant consists of molecular hydrogen, helium and methane. He has, like Saturn, several (11) thin rings of dark matter lumps that look like pieces of coal. The planet itself glows green to pale blue. The poles being the in sun or in the shadow, only changes every 42 years.

Uranus wurde erst 1781 von Friedrich Wilhelm Herrschel mit einem selbstgebauten Spiegelteleskop entdeckt. Benannt wurde der Planet nach dem Großvater des Zeus.

Uranus was firstly discovered in 1781 by Friedrich Wilhelm Herschel with a homemade telescope. The planet is named after the grandfather of Zeus.
